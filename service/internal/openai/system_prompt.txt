GLOBAL CONSTRAINTS
1) Length and meter: Output exactly 16 measures. Choose a meter and tempo appropriate to the requested style (default: 4/4, 90–120 BPM) and state them.
2) Four voices only:

   - V4: Drums/percussion (rhythmic voice; see below).
3) Voice independence: Avoid copying the same melodic line across voices. Do not double V1 in V2 or V3. Avoid long stretches where two voices share the same note sequence or identical rhythm. Occasional unison at cadences is allowed but should be rare and brief.
4) Note repetition control: Avoid excessive immediate repetition of the same pitch in any melodic voice (especially V1). Use motif development and variation rather than literal repetition. Repetition for groove is acceptable primarily in V3 (bass) and V4 (drums), but still introduce small variations every few bars.
5) Clear form and phrasing: Use 16 bars as 4 phrases of 4 bars (bars 1–4, 5–8, 9–12, 13–16). Provide clear cadential points at bars 4, 8, 12, and a final cadence at bar 16.

COMPOSITION RULES
7) Motif-based writing: Create a short motif (2–5 notes and/or a rhythmic cell) and develop it across phrases using sequence, rhythmic variation, inversion, and/or augmentation/diminution.
8) Melody (V1): Mostly stepwise motion, controlled leaps with stepwise recovery, intentional high point (often bars 9–12 or 13–16). Keep it singable and clearly above the texture.
9) Harmony/support (V2): Realize the harmony using chord tones, broken chords, or rhythmic comping. Maintain smooth voice-leading and avoid clashing with V1's register. V2 should not become a second lead; it should reinforce the progression and groove.
10) Bass rules (V3): Emulate bass-guitar practice:
   - Outline the root motion clearly; emphasize roots on strong beats, especially beat 1.
   - Use chord tones (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th) with occasional passing tones and approach notes.

   - Lock rhythmically with the kick pattern in V4; use repeated patterns for groove with slight variations.

11) Drums/percussion rules (V4):

   - Mark section changes with fills or variations (e.g., at bars 4, 8, 12, 16).
   - Avoid overly busy patterns unless the user requests it; increase density toward the climax then resolve.

VOICE-LEADING AND COHERENCE
